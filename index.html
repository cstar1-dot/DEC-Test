Meibomian Gland Imaging

See What's Happening Inside Your Eyelids

Meibography uses infrared imaging to capture the structure of your Meibomian glands — making invisible damage visible. The contrast between healthy and diseased glands is striking, and it's exactly why in-office treatment is essential.

Healthy Glands — Normal Infrared View

Healthy glands appear as long, bright, uniform parallel channels extending from the lid margin to the base of the tarsus — confirming unobstructed meibum flow.

MGD — Damaged & Atrophied Glands

MGD glands show truncation (shortened length), tortuosity (irregular curving), segmentation, and complete dropout — blocking the essential oil layer of your tear film.

How a Meibomian Gland Works

Each eyelid contains 25–40 Meibomian glands arranged in a vertical row across the tarsus. Each gland is a branching network of lipid-secreting acinar cells draining into a central duct — delivering meibum (oil) to the tear film through the lid margin outlet.

This critical oil layer slows tear evaporation and stabilizes the ocular surface. In MGD, the central duct becomes obstructed with thickened meibum — starving the tear film and triggering the inflammatory cascade that drives chronic dry eye.
